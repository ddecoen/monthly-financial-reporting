# Monthly Financial Reporting - Controller's Guide

## Overview
This document outlines the monthly financial close and reporting process for our Series C technology company. As Controller, this guide serves as the definitive reference for our month-end close procedures, deliverables, and timelines.

## Monthly Close Timeline

### Days 1-3: Month-End Activities
- **Accounts Payable/Receivable**: Cut-off procedures and accruals
- **Payroll**: Verify payroll posting and accruals for the period
- **Revenue Recognition**: Review contracts and recognize revenue per ASC 606
- **Expense Accruals**: Accrue for known expenses not yet invoiced
- **Intercompany Reconciliation**: Reconcile all intercompany transactions

### Days 4-5: Close the Books
- **General Ledger Review**: Review all GL accounts for accuracy
- **Bank Reconciliations**: Complete all bank and credit card reconciliations
- **Balance Sheet Reconciliations**: Reconcile all material balance sheet accounts
- **Journal Entries**: Post all monthly recurring and adjusting entries
- **Close Period**: Lock the accounting period in the ERP system

### Days 6-8: Financial Statement Preparation
- **Draft Financials**: Prepare draft P&L, Balance Sheet, and Cash Flow Statement
- **Variance Analysis**: Analyze actual vs. budget and prior period
- **Management Adjustments**: Review and post any required adjustments
- **Supporting Schedules**: Prepare detailed schedules for key line items

### Days 9-10: Review and Finalization
- **CFO Review**: Present preliminary results to CFO
- **Board Package Preparation**: Compile board-ready financial materials
- **Final Sign-off**: Obtain approval on final numbers

## Key Deliverables

### Internal Reporting (Due: Day 8)
- Income Statement (P&L) with budget variance
- Balance Sheet with prior period comparison
- Cash Flow Statement (direct and indirect method)
- Department-level P&L
- Key metrics dashboard (burn rate, runway, unit economics)
- Headcount and compensation analysis

### Board Reporting (Due: Day 10)
- Executive summary of financial performance
- Consolidated financial statements
- SaaS metrics (ARR, MRR, CAC, LTV, churn, NRR)
- Cash position and runway analysis
- Hiring plan vs. actuals
- KPI dashboard

### Investor Reporting (As Required)
- Cap table updates
- Covenant compliance certificates
- Quarterly investor updates with financial highlights

## Critical Accounting Areas

### Revenue Recognition (ASC 606)
- Review all new contracts for revenue recognition implications
- Track deferred revenue and unbilled revenue
- Document revenue recognition policies
- Maintain contract database with key terms

### Stock-Based Compensation (ASC 718)
- Calculate monthly stock option expense
- Track option grants and vesting schedules
- Update valuation assumptions quarterly
- Coordinate with legal on equity grants

### Capitalized Software Development (ASC 350-40)
- Track development hours for capitalization
- Review projects for capitalization criteria
- Calculate monthly amortization
- Document capitalization policies

### Cash Management
- Daily cash positioning
- Manage vendor payment timing
- Optimize cash deployment
- Monitor credit facility usage and covenants

## Common Challenges & Solutions

### Challenge: Late vendor invoices
**Solution**: Maintain strong accrual process; track open POs and contracts

### Challenge: Complex revenue recognition
**Solution**: Maintain detailed contract database; document policies; use automation tools

### Challenge: Department budget variance explanations
**Solution**: Establish monthly check-ins with department heads before close

### Challenge: Banking reconciliation delays
**Solution**: Implement daily cash reconciliation; automate bank feeds

### Challenge: Headcount tracking accuracy
**Solution**: Integrate HRIS with financial systems; weekly headcount reports

## Compliance & Controls

### Internal Controls
- Segregation of duties matrix maintained
- Monthly bank reconciliations reviewed by Controller
- Journal entry approval workflows enforced
- Access controls reviewed quarterly
- SOX compliance readiness (prepare for SOX 404 requirements post-IPO)

### External Audit Readiness
- Maintain PBC (Provided by Client) list
- Document significant accounting policies
- Prepare account rollforwards
- Maintain support for material transactions
- Annual financial statement audit (prepare for this at Series C+)
